Hummingbird

Hummingbirds are New World birds found only in the Americas, mainly South America.

  • Species: more than 340
  • Wing beat: 720 - 5400 times / minute
  • Lifespan: 3 to 5 years
  • Flight modes: hover, forwards, backwards, upside down

Hummingbirds are one of the smallest kinds of bird in the world. They are called hummingbirds due to the sound created by their rapidly beating wings. Hummingbirds drink the nectar of flowers which gives them a good source of glucose energy, they will catch insects every now and again for a protein boost.
